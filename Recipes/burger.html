Burger: How to Cook

Simple Burger

The simple burger is a timeless classic that has become a staple in many households and restaurants around the world. Known for its straightforward preparation and delicious taste, the simple burger consists of a juicy beef patty, seasoned with just salt and pepper, and cooked to perfection. It is typically served on a toasted bun with traditional toppings such as lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, and a slice of cheese. The simplicity of the ingredients allows the natural flavors of the beef to shine, making it a favorite for those who appreciate a no-fuss, satisfying meal.

Creating the perfect simple burger starts with selecting high-quality ground beef, preferably with a good fat content to ensure juiciness and flavor. The beef is then formed into patties, taking care not to overwork the meat to maintain its tenderness. Cooking the patties on a hot grill or skillet helps to achieve a nice sear on the outside while keeping the inside moist and flavorful. The simple burger's versatility allows for endless customization, whether you prefer to add a special sauce, bacon, or avocado. Despite its name, the simple burger offers a delightful and fulfilling dining experience that can be enjoyed by everyone.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • ½ cup fine dry bread crumbs

Steps

  1. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil grate.
  2. Whisk egg, salt, and pepper together in a medium bowl.
  3. Add ground beef and bread crumbs; mix with your hands or a fork until well blended.
  4. Form into four 3/4-inch-thick patties.
  5. Place patties on the preheated grill. Cover and cook 6 to 8 minutes per side, or to desired doneness. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 160 degrees F (70 degrees oC).
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
